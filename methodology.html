Project Management
Methodology

How Do We Do It?

1

Project Proposal
& Client Approval

In this initial phase, we develop a project proposal outlining the scope, objectives, and deliverables of the project. Once the proposal is ready, it is presented to the client for their review and approval. This phase sets the foundation for the project.

2

Project Preparation

After receiving approval, the project team starts preparing for the project's execution. This includes assembling the necessary resources, defining roles and responsibilities, and creating a project plan with timelines and milestones.

3

Business Blueprint

In this phase, we work closely with the client to create a detailed blueprint of the business processes that will be impacted by the project. The blueprint serves as a comprehensive guide for how the project will align with the client's business objectives.

4

Realization Design

This phase involves designing the technical and functional aspects of the project. It includes creating system designs, data models, and other technical specifications required for the project's implementation.

5

Realization Construct

Here, the actual development work begins. The project team builds and configures the systems, applications, or solutions according to the design specifications created in the previous phase. It may involve coding, customization, and integration.

6

Realization Test

After the construction phase, rigorous testing is performed to ensure that the developed systems meet the requirements and function correctly. Various testing methodologies, including unit testing, integration testing, and user acceptance testing, may be conducted.

7

Final Preparation

This phase involves finalizing all project components, ensuring that everything is in place for a successful go-live. It may include data migration, training, documentation, and performance tuning.

8

Go-Live

This is a critical phase where the project is launched and made accessible to users or customers. It marks the transition from development to production, and the new systems or processes become operational.

9

HyperCare

After the go-live, a period of hypercare or hypercare support begins. During this time, the project team closely monitors the system's performance, addresses any issues or glitches, and provides immediate support to users as they adapt to the new systems or processes.

10

Project Closure

The final phase involves officially closing the project. This includes conducting a project review, documenting lessons learned, obtaining client acceptance and sign-off, and archiving project-related materials. It marks the end of the project's life cycle.
